Cauliflower Rice Balls

Servings: 2

  • Calories: 257 | Total Fat: 11.5g
  • Carbohydrates: 15.6g | Saturated Fat: 5g
  • Cholesterol: 95.5mg| Protein: 21.5g
  • Sodium: 644mg | Sugar: 2.5g
  • Fiber: 3g
  • Prep Time: 5min
  • Cook Time: 25min
  • Total Time: 30min

Ingredients

  • 1) 1 Italian chicken sausage link, casing removed (2.75 oz / 78g)
  • 2) 2.25 cups riced cauliflower, frozen works great
  • 3) 0.25 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 4) 2 tablespoons homemade marinara (plus optional more for serving)
  • 5) 0.5 cup part skim shredded mozzarella
  • 6) 1 large egg, beaten
  • 7) 0.25 cup bread crumbs
  • 8) 1 tablespoon grated Pecorino Romano or parmesan
  • 9) Cooking spray

Steps

  • 1) Heat a medium skillet over medium-high heat. Add the sausage and cook, breaking the meat up with spoon as it cooks as small as you can, about 4 to 5 minutes.
  • 2) Add the cauliflower, salt and marinara and cook 6 minutes on medium heat, stirring until the cauliflower is tender and heated through.
  • 3) Remove from heat and add the mozzarella cheese to the skillet and stir well to mix. Let it cool 3 to 4 minutes, until it’s easy to handle.
  • 4) Spray a 0.25 cup measuring cup with cooking spray and fill with cauliflower mixture, leveling the top.
    Use a small spoon to scoop out into your palm and roll into a ball. Set aside on a dish.
  • 5) Repeat with the remaining cauliflower, you should have 6 balls.
  • 6) Place the egg in one bowl and the breadcrumbs in another.
  • 7) Add the parmesan to the crumbs and mix.
  • 8) Dip the ball in the egg, then in the crumbs and transfer to a baking sheet. Spray the top with cooking spray.
  • 9) If baking in the oven, bake 425ºF | 218ºC 25 minutes, until golden. If making in the air fryer, bake 400F for 9 minutes turning halfway until golden.

Notes

  • 1) The trick is to add the mozzarella cheese while the cauliflower is still hot so it all melts together.
    Then let it cool a few minutes before rolling them up into balls.
  • 2) For perfect balls, take 0.25 cup measuring cup, spray it with cooking spray and fill it.
    Level it, then use a small spoon to scoop it out into your palm, then roll.
  • 3) If you have problems with it sticking to your hands you can add a little oil to your hands.
  • 4) For variation, swap the sausage for turkey. Keep in mind that the sausage includes herbs for flavor.
    Add garlic, onion, basil and/or oregano to compensate.
  • 5) To help keep the ball together, try chilling the mix in the refrigerator for approx. 20min.
