Petite Chocolate Chip Banana Bread Loaves

As Loaf: Servings: 8 | Amount Per Serving: 1 loaf

  • Calories: 198 | Fat: 5g
  • Protein: 4g | Carbohydrates: 41g
  • Fiber: 4g | Sugar: 22g
  • Sodium: 222mg

As Muffins: Servings: 12 muffins | Amount Per Serving: 1 muffin

  • Calories: 132 | Fat: 3.5g
  • Protein: 2.8g | Carbohydrates: 27.2g
  • Fiber: 2.7g | Sugar: 14.6g
  • Sodium: 148.6mg
  • Prep Time: 20mins
  • Cook Time: 40mins
  • Total Time: 60mins

Ingredients

  • 1) 3 ripe medium bananas
  • 2) 1.5 oz mini chocolate chips
  • 3) 1.25 cups white whole wheat flour
  • 4) 3/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 5) 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 6) 2 tablespoon butter, softened
  • 7) 1/3 cup brown sugar
  • 8) 2 large egg whites
  • 9) 1/3 cup unsweetened apple sauce
  • 10)1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Steps

  • 1) Preheat oven to 325°. Spray a non-stick loaf pan with cooking spray.
  • 2) Mash bananas in a bowl, set aside.
  • 3) In a medium bowl, combine flour, baking soda and salt with a wire whisk. Set aside.
  • 4) In a large bowl cream butter and sugar with an electric mixer.
  • 5) Add egg whites, bananas, apple sauce, vanilla; beat at medium speed until thick. Scrape down sides of the bowl.
  • 6) Add flour mixture, then blend at low speed until combined. Fold in chocolate chips. Do not over mix.
  • 7) Pour batter into muffin pan; bake on the center rack for about 40 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.

Notes

  • 1) These make 8 large muffins in comparison to using a normal sized muffin tin which gives you 12-13.
    If you don’t have these Petite Loaf Pans you can use one large 8 x 5-inch loaf instead,
    cooking time will be closer to 50-60 minutes or use a regular sized muffin tin, baking them for about 25 minutes.
  • 2) Add nuts or cinnamon for added flavor.
  • 3) Butter can be substituted for olive oil.
