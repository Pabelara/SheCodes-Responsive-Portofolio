Chicken Romano Meatballs

Servings: 4 | Amount Per Serving: 6 meatballs with sauce

  • Calories: 295 | Total Fat: 13g
  • Carbohydrates: 16g | Saturated Fat: 3g
  • Sugar: 2g | Protein: 26g
  • Sodium: 756mg | Cholesterol: 149mg
  • Fiber: 2g
  • Prep Time: 20mins
  • Cook Time: 40mins
  • Total Time: 1hr

Ingredients

    Meatballs

  • 1) 1lb (454g) ground chicken
  • 2) 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3) 1 large egg, lightly beaten
  • 4) 0.5 cup seasoned bread crumbs, or gluten-free crumbs
  • 5) ⅓ cup Romano cheese
  • 6) 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 7) 1/2 teaspoon dried basil
  • 8) 0.5 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 9) 0.5 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
  • 10) 0.5 teaspoon salt
  • 11) 0.5 teaspoon pepper

Sauce

  • 1) 1 shallot, diced
  • 2) 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3) Salt
  • 4) 1/3 cup dry white wine, like chardonnay
  • 5) 1 cup chicken broth
  • 6) 1 lemon, sliced

Steps

  • 1) Preheat the oven to 350ºF | 177ºC.
  • 2) In a bowl, combine the ground chicken with the garlic, egg, bread crumbs, cheese, parsley, dried basil, oregano, pepper flakes, salt and pepper.
  • 3) Mix with clean hands until just combined - do not overmix.
  • 4) Shape the mixture into meatballs that are 1-inch in size - or slightly less, about 24.
  • 5) Heat an oven-safe skillet over medium heat and add 1 tablespoon of olive oil.
  • 6) Add the meatballs in a single layer (you may need to do this in batches), browning on all sides, cooking about 5 to 6 minutes in total.
    You don’t need to cook them fully because we will put them in the oven - just brown them.
  • 7) Transfer the meatballs to a plate.
  • 8) In the same skillet over medium heat, add the shallots and garlic with a pinch of salt and stir.
  • 9) Cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Stir in the white wine, scraping any brown bits from the bottom of the pan with a wooden spoon.
  • 10) Let it simmer for 2 minutes. Add in the sliced lemons and chicken stock.
  • 11) Add the meatballs back into the pan.
  • 12) Bake for 15 minutes, or until the meatballs are cooked through to 165ºF | 74ºC.

Notes

  • 1) Consider doubling sauce recipe if adding to pasta.
  • 2) Let meatballs rest in the fridge for a couple of hours to ensure they remain firm.
  • 3) It's possible to finish the dish on the stove instead of in the oven.
